Economic Shifts and Structural Changes: Urban areas often experience economic shifts and structural changes that can lead to job losses or a mismatch between available skills and job demands. Industries may decline or relocate due to globalization, technological advancements, or changes in consumer preferences, resulting in the loss of traditional manufacturing or industrial jobs that once formed the backbone of urban economies. Additionally, rapid urbanization can outpace the creation of new job opportunities, leading to unemployment or underemployment, especially among vulnerable populations with limited access to education or training programs that align with emerging industries. As a result, urban areas may struggle to adapt to changing economic landscapes, leaving many residents without viable employment options

Rapid urbanization in developing countries has led to the emergence of sprawling informal settlements characterized by inadequate housing, insufficient infrastructure, and limited access to basic services such as clean water, sanitation, and healthcare. This phenomenon has profound implications for the well-being and livelihoods of millions of urban residents, exacerbating poverty, inequality, and social exclusion. Moreover, informal settlements often lack effective urban planning and governance mechanisms, resulting in environmental degradation, heightened vulnerability to natural disasters, and increased strain on urban resources and ecosystems. Addressing the multifaceted challenges of informal urbanization requires holistic approaches that prioritize inclusive and sustainable urban development, equitable access to essential services, and empowerment of marginalized communities. By fostering collaboration among governments, urban planners, community organizations, and other stakeholders, interventions can be designed to improve living conditions, enhance resilience, and promote social justice in rapidly urbanizing areas.
